Layout

abKey is arranged in alphabetical order, in logical and purposeful groups. The most common keys are grouped and darkened: left-hand side are vowels AEIOU, on the righthand side: RST, the most common consonants, and the common Space and Return keys. The less common keys are in lighter shade, but also arranged in easy to remember groupsBCD, FGH, JK, LMN, PQ, VW, XYZ - and related to AEIOU and RST (the home rows). Generally and visibly, the keys are in order from A (extreme left) to Z (extreme right.) They’re also in straight rows and columns to improve accuracy.

Keyboard Settings Key

Enter Settings by clicking the key left of the Spacebar. You may enable or disable T+ and Tr, and access the rT+ Manager which allows you to see, edit, replace and delete your stored data. You may also select or deselect functions for Capitals and Spelling.

Super Keys: T+ and Tr

There are 2 super keys unique to abKey:
T+, Tr
T+ allows you to store data in 5 bins in the Lite Version and up to 58 bins in Premium.
Step 1: Click T+ followed by an alphabet/ number/or accented character key
Step 2: A text box opens up for you to type or
paste your text. If you get the message “This key slot is not empty” and continue typing,
and SAVE, you’ll be over-writing the stored data in the bin.
Or click CANCEL and choose another bin. [NOTE: Premium allows you to enter new data without over-writing previous entries; and selectively retrieve from multiple entries in a bin.]
Tr allows you to retrieve data that you’ve stored. Click Tr followed by the alphabet/ number/accented character key indicating the bin containing your data. Your data will appear at the cursor.
